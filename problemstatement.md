# project: Food waste reduction marketplace
## problem title: Food waste and inefficient redistribution of surplus food in Australia
 
##Problem description
A lot of usable food are wasted by restaurants, grocery stores and café on a daily basis dur to over production, 
short-shelf life and being unsold. A lot of these food are thrown out even though it is safe and edible. Many consumers, particularly students and low-income individuals, seek affordable food options. However, there is currently no simple, widely accessible platform that connects food businesses with consumers in real time to efficiently redistribute surplus food. This gap leads to unnecessary food waste, economic losses for businesses, and missed opportunities to improve affordable food access.
##Target Users
•	Small-to-medium food businesses like restaurants, cafés, bakeries, grocery stores
•	Budget-conscious consumers such as students, families, low-income individuals 
•	Environmentally conscious consumers seeking sustainable choices 
##Current Solutions
•	Manual discounting or end-of-day sales in stores 
•	Food donation to charities 
•	Limited third-party apps for surplus food sales 
•	Informal social media listings 
##Why Current Solutions Fail
•	Lack of real-time visibility for consumers 
•	Inefficient and inconsistent processes 
•	Limited accessibility for small businesses 
•	Poor inventory and time management for perishable items 
•	Low awareness and adoption of existing platforms 
##Proposed Solution
Creating  a web-based Food Waste Reduction Marketplace where companies may display excess food goods with
  time-limited availability and discounted prices, and customers can explore, reserve, and buy items with ease.
  To guarantee effective and fast transactions, the platform will have features including user accounts, food listings, filtering, order management, and pickup scheduling.
