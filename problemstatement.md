## Problem Statement

### Background
Food waste is a significant and ongoing issue in Australia, with large quantities of edible food discarded daily by restaurants, cafés, supermarkets, and other food businesses. Much of this waste occurs due to unsold inventory at the end of the day, strict food safety regulations, and lack of efficient redistribution channels. 

At the same time, many individuals—including students, low-income households, and environmentally conscious consumers—face challenges in accessing affordable and sustainable food options. This creates a clear imbalance between the availability of surplus food and the needs of potential consumers.

---

### Problem Description
Despite the existence of surplus food, there is currently no widely adopted, efficient, and real-time system that connects food providers with consumers or charities. Businesses often lack the tools to quickly and effectively advertise surplus food before it expires. As a result, edible food is discarded even when there is demand for it.

Consumers and charities also face difficulties in identifying available surplus food in their area. Existing processes are often manual, time-sensitive, and poorly coordinated, leading to missed opportunities for redistribution.

---

### Stakeholders
The problem affects multiple groups:

- **Food Businesses**  
  Experience financial losses due to unsold food and lack effective methods to recover value from surplus items.

- **Consumers (Students, Low-Income Individuals)**  
  Miss opportunities to access affordable food options that could reduce their cost of living.

- **Charities and NGOs**  
  Encounter challenges in sourcing and collecting food donations in a timely and efficient manner.

- **Environment**  
  Food waste contributes to landfill overflow and greenhouse gas emissions, negatively impacting sustainability efforts.

---

### Limitations of Existing Solutions
Current approaches to addressing food waste include end-of-day discounts, donation programs, and third-party applications. However, these solutions have several limitations:

- **Lack of Real-Time Visibility**  
  Users are not able to see up-to-date information about available surplus food.

- **Limited Accessibility**  
  Many solutions are location-dependent or require physical presence.

- **Inefficient Processes**  
  Coordination between businesses, consumers, and charities is often manual and time-consuming.

- **Low User Awareness and Engagement**  
  Existing platforms may not have a large or active user base, reducing their effectiveness.

---

### Impact
The absence of an efficient food redistribution system leads to several negative outcomes:

- Increased levels of food waste  
- Financial losses for food businesses  
- Limited access to affordable food for consumers  
- Missed opportunities for charitable redistribution  
- Environmental damage due to excess waste and emissions  

---

### Proposed Solution
To address these challenges, this project proposes the development of a web-based Food Waste Reduction Marketplace. The platform will enable businesses to list surplus food in real time, including details such as price, quantity, and expiry time.

Users will be able to search and filter listings based on location and preferences, and reserve or purchase items before they are discarded. The system aims to improve visibility, streamline coordination, and encourage active participation from all stakeholders.

By providing a centralized and user-friendly platform, the solution seeks to reduce food waste, support businesses, improve food accessibility, and contribute to environmental sustainability.
