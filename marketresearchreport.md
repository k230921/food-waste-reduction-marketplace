## Market Research Report

### Overview
The food waste reduction space has seen the emergence of several platforms aimed at redistributing surplus food. These platforms primarily focus on connecting businesses or individuals with consumers or charities. While they provide valuable services, there are still notable gaps in functionality, accessibility, and overall impact.

---

### Top Competitors

- **Too Good To Go**  
  A popular app that allows users to purchase surplus food from restaurants and stores at discounted prices.

- **OLIO**  
  A community-based platform focused on sharing surplus food between individuals and local businesses.

- **Karma**  
  Enables restaurants and cafes to sell excess food at reduced prices to nearby users.

- **Flashfood**  
  Partners with grocery stores to offer discounted food items that are close to expiry.

- **Food Rescue US**  
  Focuses on redistributing surplus food to charities and communities in need through volunteer networks.

---

### Key Features Across Competitors
Most existing platforms provide similar core functionalities:

- Listing of surplus food items  
- Discounted purchase options  
- Location-based search and discovery  
- Food donation and redistribution options  
- User ratings and reviews for trust and quality  

These features help reduce food waste but are often limited in scope and integration.

---

### Unsolved Problems
Despite the availability of these platforms, several challenges remain:

- **Lack of Real-Time Tracking**  
  Many platforms do not provide accurate, real-time updates on food availability or status.

- **Limited Geographic Coverage**  
  Services are often concentrated in major cities, with minimal support for smaller or regional areas.

- **No Impact Tracking**  
  Users and businesses cannot easily track how much food waste they have reduced or their environmental impact.

- **Fragmented Functionality**  
  Existing solutions typically focus on either selling or donating food, rather than providing a unified platform.

- **User Engagement Challenges**  
  Some platforms struggle to maintain active user participation and awareness.

---

### Our Unique Value Proposition
This project introduces a unified platform that addresses the limitations of current solutions.

**One platform to list, buy, and donate surplus food while tracking waste reduction impact end-to-end.**

Key differentiators include:

- Real-time updates on food availability  
- Support for both commercial transactions and donations  
- Impact tracking (e.g., food saved, environmental benefits)  
- Broader accessibility for different user groups  
- A centralized system that integrates multiple functionalities into a single platform  

---

### Conclusion
While existing platforms have made progress in addressing food waste, there is still a need for a more comprehensive, real-time, and user-focused solution. This project aims to fill that gap by offering an integrated marketplace that improves efficiency, accessibility, and measurable impact.
